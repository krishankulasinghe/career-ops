How It Works — The Basic Workflow

Every job search in Career-Ops follows the same loop. Here it is from start to finish:

1

Sign in

Enter your email and password on the login screen. You land on the Dashboard automatically.

The login screen — enter your credentials and click Sign in
2

Set up your Profile

Before evaluating any job, go to Profile and fill in your Target Roles and Compensation tabs. The AI reads these every time it scores a job. Without them, scores will be inaccurate.

💡
Target Roles is the most important field. Add the job titles you are actively looking for — e.g. "Senior Software Engineer", "Staff Engineer". You can add multiple.
Profile → Identity tab. Use the tabs at the top to fill in Target Roles and Compensation.
3

Evaluate a job

Click New Evaluation in the sidebar. Paste the job posting URL (or the full job description text if you don't have a URL) and click Evaluate.

New Evaluation — paste the URL or job description text, then click Evaluate
⏱
Evaluation takes about 30–90 seconds. The AI fetches the full job description, scores it against your profile across six dimensions, and generates a report.
4

Read the score and report

When the evaluation finishes, open Applications in the sidebar. Click the new row to open the full report.

The score is out of 5.0 and covers six areas: role fit, compensation match, company quality, location/remote policy, growth potential, and legitimacy.

4.5–5Excellent match — prioritise this application
4.0–4.4Strong match — definitely worth applying
3.5–3.9Decent fit — apply if you have bandwidth
3.0–3.4Weak fit — consider carefully before applying
0–2.9Poor fit — not recommended
5

Track your application

The job appears automatically in Applications with status Evaluated. As you progress through the hiring process, update the status: Applied → Interview → Offer (or Rejected / Discarded). Your dashboard stats update in real time.

Applications — every evaluated job appears here with its score and current status

Features & When to Use Them

Each feature below explains what it does, when to use it, and exactly what to click — step by step. You don't need all of them on day one, but read through so you know what's available.

🏠

Dashboard

Your job search at a glance — the first screen you see after login
Start here

The Dashboard is your home screen. It shows live summary numbers — how many jobs you have evaluated, how many applications are active, your average score, and how many interviews you have lined up. You don't type anything here; it just shows you where you stand.

Dashboard — KPI cards update automatically as you evaluate jobs and move applications forward
What you'll see: Four or more stat cards (Total Evaluated, Applied, In Interview, Average Score). As you add more evaluations and update statuses, these numbers update in real time.
💡
Check the Dashboard at the start of each session to see where your search stands. If your average score is dropping, your Profile targeting may need adjustment.
⭐

New Evaluation

Have the AI score any job against your profile in under 90 seconds
Most used

This is the core feature. You give Career-Ops a job posting — either as a URL or by pasting the text — and the AI reads it, compares it against everything in your Profile, and produces a detailed report with a score out of 5.0. The report tells you exactly where the job is a good fit and where the gaps are.

New Evaluation page — paste a job URL or switch to the text tab to paste a description directly
How to evaluate a job — step by step
  1. Click New Evaluation in the left sidebar.
  2. If you have a URL (from LinkedIn, a company website, or a job board): paste it into the Job URL field.
  3. If you only have the job description text (no URL): click the Paste Text tab and paste the full description there instead.
  4. Optionally add a note about the company or why you are interested — this gets saved with the report.
  5. Click the blue Evaluate button.
  6. The page will show a spinner and a status message. Do not close the tab — wait 30–90 seconds.
  7. When the evaluation finishes, you are taken to the report. Read the score, the six dimension ratings, and the gap analysis.
  8. The job is automatically added to your Applications list with status Evaluated.
What you'll see in the report: An overall score (e.g. 4.2 / 5.0), sub-scores for Role Fit, Compensation, Company Quality, Location/Remote, Growth Potential, and Legitimacy. Below that: a list of strengths, gaps, and a recommended action (Apply / Skip / Consider).
⚠️
Your Profile must be filled in first. If Target Roles and Compensation are empty, the AI has nothing to compare the job against and the score will be meaningless. Set up your Profile before running your first evaluation.
📋

Applications

Every job you've evaluated, in one sortable list — update status as you progress
Check daily

Applications is your master tracker. Every time you run an evaluation, the job appears here automatically. You then update the status as you move through the hiring process — from Evaluated, to Applied, to Interview, to Offer or Rejected. This is also where you reopen past reports.

Applications list — each row is one job with its company, role, score, current status, and date
How to use the Applications tracker
  1. Click Applications in the left sidebar to open your list.
  2. Each row shows: Company, Role, Score, Status, and Date. Click any row to open the full evaluation report.
  3. To update a status (e.g. after you applied): click the row, find the Status field, and change it to Applied.
  4. Continue updating as things progress: Applied → Interview → Offer or Applied → Rejected.
  5. Use the Search bar at the top to find a specific company quickly.
  6. Use the Filter by Status dropdown to see only active applications, or only rejections.
Status options explained: Evaluated = report done, no action yet. Applied = you sent the application. Interview = in the process. Offer = received an offer. Rejected = rejected by company. Discarded = you chose not to pursue it.
💡
Keep statuses up to date. The Dashboard summary numbers and Analytics charts are driven entirely by what you set here — stale statuses give you a misleading picture of your search.
📥

Pipeline

A URL inbox — save jobs while browsing, evaluate them later in one batch

The Pipeline is a holding area. When you are browsing LinkedIn or a job board and find something interesting but don't have time to evaluate it right now, paste the URL into the Pipeline. Later, when you sit down to do a proper review, you can process all saved URLs at once instead of opening them one by one.

Pipeline — a list of saved job URLs waiting to be evaluated. Click Process All to evaluate them in bulk.
How to save jobs to the Pipeline and process them
  1. When you spot an interesting job but don't want to evaluate it right now, copy the job URL.
  2. Click Pipeline in the sidebar.
  3. Paste the URL into the input field at the top and click Add. The job appears in the list.
  4. Repeat for as many jobs as you want to save.
  5. When you are ready to evaluate them, click Process All. Each URL is sent to the evaluation queue one by one.
  6. Check Applications a few minutes later — all processed jobs will appear there with their scores.
💡
Use Pipeline during your morning browse, then process everything in one go later. It keeps your evaluation sessions focused instead of scattered.
🔭

Portals

Tell the Scanner which companies to watch and what job titles to look for
Set up once

Portals is the configuration screen for the Scanner. It holds a list of companies (with their job portal URLs) and a set of keyword filters. You set this up once, and then the Scanner uses it every time it runs. Think of it as telling Career-Ops: "watch these 20 companies and alert me if they post anything matching these titles."

Portals — each row is a company with its portal URL and the title keywords to match against
How to set up your Portals
  1. Click Portals in the sidebar.
  2. You will see a pre-loaded list of companies. Scroll through and toggle Enable on the ones you actually care about. Toggle off companies that are not relevant to your search.
  3. For each enabled portal, check the Title Filter column. This is the keyword the Scanner will use to match job titles. Make sure it reflects what you are looking for — e.g. "Engineer", "Product Manager", "Data Scientist".
  4. To add a company that isn't listed: click + Add Portal, enter the company name and their careers page URL, and set the title filter.
  5. Click Save when done.
💡
Use broad title keywords. "Engineer" will catch "Senior Engineer", "Staff Engineer", "Software Engineer II" — all in one filter. Narrow keywords like "Senior Software Engineer" may miss variations.
⚠️
At least one portal must be enabled with a title filter set before the Scanner can run. If the Scanner returns no results, come back here first and check your filters.
🔍

Scanner

Automatically find new openings at your target companies — uses zero AI credits
Free to run

The Scanner hits the job portal APIs of every company in your Portals list and checks for new openings that match your title filters. It does this without using any AI — it directly queries Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby, and other ATS APIs. Results appear instantly. You then decide which ones to evaluate.

Scanner — click Scan Now, then results appear below. Send any interesting job to Evaluation or Pipeline.
How to run a scan and act on results
  1. Make sure your Portals are configured first (see above). The Scanner will only check enabled portals.
  2. Click Scanner in the sidebar.
  3. Click the Scan Now button. The scan usually completes in 10–30 seconds.
  4. Results appear in a list below the button. Each result shows the company, job title, location, and a link to the original posting.
  5. For each result you are interested in, click Evaluate to send it straight to AI evaluation, or Save to Pipeline to review it later.
  6. Jobs you have already seen are marked as Previously seen — the Scanner remembers what it showed you and won't show duplicates.
✅
Run the Scanner weekly. It costs nothing and takes under 30 seconds. You will catch new postings before other applicants, which matters — job postings get the most views in the first 48 hours.

Analytics

Charts and patterns that show you what is and isn't working in your search
After 10+ jobs

Analytics turns your Applications data into charts. You can see your pipeline funnel (how many jobs move from Evaluated to Applied to Interview), your score distribution, rejection patterns, and which companies or role types are scoring highest. It helps you answer the question: "Am I targeting the right kinds of jobs?"

Analytics — funnel chart, score distribution, and rejection pattern analysis
How to read the Analytics page
  1. Click Analytics in the sidebar.
  2. The Funnel chart at the top shows how many jobs are in each status — this tells you if you are applying to enough jobs or if too many are stalling at Evaluated.
  3. The Score Distribution chart shows the spread of your scores. If most scores are below 3.5, your target role keywords or compensation range may need adjusting.
  4. The Rejection Patterns section (visible once you have some rejections) shows whether rejections cluster by company type, role level, or location.
  5. Use the date range filter at the top to focus on a specific time period.
💡
Analytics is most useful once you have 10 or more evaluations. With fewer data points, the patterns are too noisy to draw conclusions from.
🎤

Interview Prep

Build a library of STAR stories so you're never caught off guard in an interview

Interview Prep is a personal story bank. A STAR story is a structured way to answer behavioural interview questions ("Tell me about a time when..."). You write the story once — Situation, Task, Action, Result — and store it here. Before each interview you review the relevant stories instead of trying to recall them under pressure.

Interview Prep — your saved STAR stories, tagged by competency so you can find the right one quickly
How to build your story bank
  1. Click Interview Prep in the sidebar.
  2. Click + Add Story.
  3. Give the story a title that describes the situation (e.g. "Led migration to microservices under 3-month deadline").
  4. Fill in the four STAR fields: Situation (context), Task (what you had to do), Action (exactly what you did), Result (outcome, ideally with a number).
  5. Add a Competency tag — e.g. Leadership, Problem Solving, Communication. This helps you filter later.
  6. Click Save. Repeat for each story.
  7. Aim for 8–12 stories covering different competencies. Good coverage: 2–3 leadership stories, 2–3 technical problem-solving, 1–2 conflict/stakeholder, 1–2 delivery under pressure.
Before an interview: Filter by the competencies the company is likely to test. Read through your top 3–4 stories for each. You will answer confidently because you practised the structure in advance, not on the spot.
💡
Add a story immediately after any experience that was challenging or had a measurable outcome — your memory is sharpest right after the event. Don't wait until interview season.
👤

Profile

The information the AI uses to score every job — keep it accurate and complete
Do this first

Your Profile is what the AI reads before scoring any job. It contains your target roles, compensation expectations, location preferences, and a narrative about your strengths. The more complete and accurate your Profile, the more accurate your evaluation scores will be.

Profile — four tabs: Identity, Target Roles, Compensation, and Narrative. Fill in all four for best results.
How to fill in your Profile
  1. Click Profile in the sidebar (usually near the bottom, under your name).
  2. Identity tab: Enter your full name, current location, and years of experience. This gives the AI context about who you are.
  3. Target Roles tab: Click + Add Role and type the job titles you are actively targeting. Add multiple — e.g. "Senior Software Engineer", "Staff Engineer", "Engineering Lead". These are compared directly against job titles in every evaluation.
  4. Compensation tab: Set your minimum and target salary. Enter the currency and whether these are annual figures. The AI will flag jobs that pay below your minimum as a compensation mismatch.
  5. Narrative tab: Write 2–3 sentences about your professional superpower — the thing you do better than most candidates. Also list your must-have requirements (e.g. "fully remote", "no more than 10% travel"). The AI uses this when assessing role fit.
  6. Click Save on each tab after filling it in.
⚠️
Updating your Profile does not re-score existing evaluations. If you make significant changes, re-evaluate your top jobs to get updated scores.

Team

Invite a career coach, recruiter, or trusted colleague to collaborate on your search

Team lets you add other people to your Career-Ops workspace. A collaborator can view your evaluations and applications, leave comments, and help you decide which jobs to prioritise. You stay in control — you choose what access level each person gets.

Team page — invite people by email, set their role (Viewer or Collaborator), and manage existing members
How to invite a team member
  1. Click Team in the sidebar.
  2. Click Invite Member.
  3. Enter the person's email address.
  4. Choose their role: Viewer (can only read, not change anything) or Collaborator (can add comments and suggestions).
  5. Click Send Invite. They will receive an email with a link to join your workspace.
  6. Once they accept, they appear in the member list and can access your Applications and evaluations.
💡
If you have a career coach or a friend in the industry who reviews your applications, give them Viewer access. They can see your scores and reports without you having to export or share screenshots manually.
⚙️

Settings

Manage your account, API keys, usage quota, and organisation details

Settings is where you manage the administrative side of your account — your subscription plan, monthly usage, organisation name, and API keys if you are integrating Career-Ops with other tools. Most users only visit Settings once during setup and occasionally to check quota.

Settings — organisation details, plan information, usage meter, and API key management
Things to do in Settings
  1. Click Settings in the sidebar (at the very bottom).
  2. Check the Usage section to see how many evaluations you have used this month and how many remain in your plan.
  3. To create an API key (for developers): click API Keys → Create New Key, give it a name, and copy the key — it is shown only once.
  4. To update your organisation name or billing email: edit the fields in the Organisation section and click Save.
  5. To change your plan: click Upgrade Plan or contact support.
💡
Check Settings if you get a "quota exceeded" error when trying to evaluate a job. If you are near the limit, consider whether the jobs in your Pipeline are worth using credits on, or wait for the next billing cycle.

Important Rules, Limits & Best Practices

⚠️
Complete your Profile first. If Target Roles and Compensation are empty, the AI has nothing to compare the job against — scores will be generic and unreliable.
🚫
Don't apply to jobs scoring below 4.0 unless you have a specific reason. Career-Ops is designed for quality over quantity — a targeted application to a 4.5 beats ten sprayed applications to 3.0s.
💡
The Scanner is free. It hits company job portal APIs directly and uses zero AI credits. Run it as often as you like without worrying about quota.

Monthly usage limits (Pro plan)

Each AI evaluation counts against your monthly quota. Check Settings to see how many you have used and how many remain. The Scanner, Pipeline, and Applications tracker do not consume quota.

Each job is evaluated once

Submitting the same URL a second time creates a new evaluation and uses a credit. Before re-evaluating, check if the job is already in your Applications list.

Profile updates affect future evaluations only

Changing your Target Roles or Compensation will not re-score existing applications. If you significantly update your profile, you may want to re-evaluate key jobs.

Never submit before reviewing

Career-Ops can draft applications and fill forms, but it will always stop before clicking Submit. You make the final call on every application.

Troubleshooting Common Problems

The score seems too low / doesn't match my experience
Open Profile → Target Roles and confirm your target job titles are set correctly. The AI scores against these — if they are empty or mismatched, the score will be wrong. Also check Profile → Narrative to make sure your headline and superpowers are filled in.
My evaluation is stuck on "processing" for more than 3 minutes
Go to AI Settings and click Test Connection. If it fails, your AI provider API key may have expired or hit its rate limit. Check that the key is valid and the provider is reachable. You can also try switching to a fallback provider.
The Scanner found no results
Go to Portals and check that: (1) at least one portal is enabled, and (2) the title filter contains keywords that match real job titles at those companies. Try broadening the filter — e.g. replace "Senior Software Engineer" with just "Engineer".
I can't log in — my password is not working
Clear your browser cookies for localhost:5173 and try again. If you are the demo user, the credentials are demo@example.com / demo1234. If you are on a real account, use the Create one link on the login page to check your registration.
An application shows the wrong status
Open Applications, click the row, and update the status manually. If the status field shows something unexpected (e.g. "Evaluated" after you applied), it was not updated after you sent the application — the tracker only knows what you tell it.
I submitted the same job twice by accident
Both evaluations count against your quota and cannot be refunded. Before evaluating, quickly check Applications to confirm the job is not already there. You can delete the duplicate row from the Applications list.
